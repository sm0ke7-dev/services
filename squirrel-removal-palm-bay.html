How to Tell If You Have Squirrels

Recognizing signs of squirrels in attic spaces early prevents extensive property damage and health risks. The most obvious indicators include persistent scratching, scampering, or chattering sounds emanating from ceiling areas, particularly during dawn and dusk hours when squirrels are most active.

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Squirrel Infestation Signs

  • Audible evidence - Scratching, scampering, or chattering sounds
  • Visual droppings - Small, dark droppings near nesting areas
  • Gnaw marks - Visible damage on wooden structures or electrical components
  • Nesting debris - Shredded insulation, paper, or fabric materials
  • Exterior damage - Damaged bird feeders and disturbed garden areas

Physical evidence includes distinctive squirrel infestation signs such as small, dark droppings scattered near nesting areas, visible gnaw marks on wooden structures or electrical components, and scattered nesting debris including shredded insulation, paper, or fabric materials. Damaged bird feeders, disturbed garden areas, or evidence of squirrels around your property's exterior often indicates indoor infestation potential.

Visual confirmation through seeing squirrels entering or exiting your home provides definitive evidence requiring immediate professional intervention. Staining on exterior walls below entry points, damaged roof vents or soffit areas, and accumulating debris around your home's foundation all indicate active squirrel problems requiring expert assessment.
