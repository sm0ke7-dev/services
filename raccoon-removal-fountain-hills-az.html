Why Raccoons Are a Problem in Fountain Hills

Raccoon damage Fountain Hills homeowners experience ranges from minor nuisances to severe structural destruction and health hazards. These nocturnal mammals are remarkably intelligent and dexterous, capable of opening latches, tearing through roof materials, and creating extensive entry points throughout your property. Once established in an attic or crawl space, raccoons cause insulation damage, chew through electrical wiring, destroy HVAC ductwork, and leave behind contaminated waste that poses serious health risks.

The raccoon health risks associated with infestations in Fountain Hills are significant and should never be underestimated. Raccoons are primary carriers of rabies in Arizona, and their feces can contain raccoon roundworm (Baylisascaris procyonis), a parasite that can cause severe neurological damage in humans if accidentally ingested. Their urine and droppings also harbor leptospirosis bacteria, which can cause serious illness through contact with contaminated surfaces or inhaling airborne particles.
