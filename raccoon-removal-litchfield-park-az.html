Why Raccoons Are a Problem in Litchfield Park

Understanding why raccoon damage Litchfield Park properties face helps homeowners recognize early warning signs and appreciate the value of professional wildlife control. These medium-sized mammals possess remarkable problem-solving abilities and physical dexterity that enable them to overcome most amateur exclusion attempts. Their hand-like front paws manipulate objects with surprising precision, allowing them to open latches, tear through materials, and create substantial entry points in seemingly secure structures.

Common entry points raccoons exploit in Litchfield Park homes include roof intersections where different planes meet, soffit areas along roof edges, chimney openings without proper caps, attic vents with damaged or missing screens, and foundation gaps near air conditioning lines or utility penetrations. Once raccoons establish entry points, they typically enlarge openings through persistent gnawing and tearing, creating damage that worsens rapidly and compromises your home's weather protection and structural integrity.

Raccoon health risks demand serious attention from Litchfield Park homeowners, as these animals carry numerous diseases transmissible to humans and pets. Rabies remains the most concerning threat in Arizona, where raccoons represent a primary rabies vector species. Additional health hazards include raccoon roundworm (Baylisascaris procyonis), a dangerous parasite found in raccoon feces that can cause severe neurological damage if accidentally ingested. Leptospirosis, giardia, and various bacterial infections also spread through contact with raccoon urine, feces, or contaminated surfaces.
