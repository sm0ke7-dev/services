Health Risks of Raccoon Infestation

Raccoons carry numerous zoonotic diseases that can transmit to humans, making professional intervention crucial. Rabies, raccoon roundworm, leptospirosis, and salmonella are among the raccoon dangers that threaten your family's health. These health risks multiply when raccoon droppings accumulate in attics, walls, or other areas of your home.

‚ö†Ô∏è Serious Health Risks from Raccoons

  • Rabies transmission - Fatal viral infection affecting the nervous system
  • Raccoon roundworm - Parasitic infection causing severe neurological damage
  • Leptospirosis - Bacterial infection affecting kidneys and liver
  • Salmonella poisoning - Gastrointestinal illness from contaminated droppings
  • Histoplasmosis - Respiratory infection from airborne spores

Beyond disease transmission, raccoons cause extensive property damage through their foraging and nesting behaviors. They tear insulation, chew electrical wires, damage roofing materials, and contaminate areas with urine and feces. The longer an infestation persists, the more expensive repairs become, making immediate professional removal a cost-effective investment.
